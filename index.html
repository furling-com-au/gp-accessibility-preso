Evidence Gap in Australian GP Accessibility

International Context

  • Established methods: E2SFCA widely used globally for healthcare accessibility
  • Appointment metrics: Wait time standards established in US, UK, Canada
  • Primary care impact: Strong evidence linking GP access to population health outcomes

Methodological Evolution

  • Provider ratios → 2SFCA → E2SFCA
  • Distance decay and population competition
  • Capacity weighting: headcount → FTE → appointments

Australian Gap

  • No appointment-weighted E2SFCA for Australian GP accessibility
  • Current DPA: Binary classification at SA3 level
  • Existing studies use headcount or FTE, not actual appointment availability

Why This Matters

  • Working Better for Medicare Review calls for gradient approach
  • Patient experience = appointment availability, not just GP presence
  • Within-area heterogeneity invisible to current metrics
Research Opportunity: First appointment-weighted E2SFCA application for Australian GP access

Research Questions & Hypotheses

Primary Research Question

To what extent does appointment-weighted E2SFCA reveal intra-area heterogeneity
in GP accessibility within DPA-designated regions?

Q1: Hidden variation?

H1: ≥30% of SA1s in DPA areas have adequate access despite blanket shortage designation

Q2: Urban complexity impact?

H2: Intersection density explains 20-30% of accessibility variation in urban areas

Q3: Binary vs gradient?

H3: Continuous accessibility scores better capture actual access patterns than binary DPA status

Q4: Geographic patterns?

H4: Access drivers differ: urban (competition), regional (distance), remote (absolute scarcity)

The E2SFCA Method - Complete Process

Step 1: Supply Ratios

Rj = Appts ÷ Weighted Pop

Step 2: Sum Access

Ai = Σ(Rj × weight)

Step 3: Classify

Higher scores = Better access
Medium scores = Limited access
Low scores = GP desert
(Thresholds to be validated)

The Urban Paradox

Short physical distances in cities can mean long travel times due to traffic and public transport gaps

The Rural Reality

Vast distances with limited alternative options - the "next closest" GP may be hours away

Why One Size Doesn't Fit All

Travel tolerance varies dramatically: What's acceptable in remote areas would be considered inaccessible in cities. This is why E2SFCA needs different decay parameters (σ) for different contexts - but determining these values requires validation against actual utilization patterns.
